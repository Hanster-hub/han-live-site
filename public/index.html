About

I'm a robotics researcher at Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD), where my work sits at the intersection of sensor fusion, localisation, and autonomous systems. I focus on enabling robots to know precisely where they are — and where their teammates are — even when GPS is unavailable.

My primary research thread is Ultra-Wideband (UWB) ranging: characterising non-line-of-sight (NLOS) errors, building neural network models to mitigate them, and fusing UWB with LiDAR and odometry to produce robust position estimates. A second thread covers multi-robot SLAM — coordinating distributed map-building and relative localisation across robot teams.

I've published 10 peer-reviewed papers (IEEE IROS, CASE, ROBIO, IEEE/ASME AIM; journals Digital Signal Processing and Mechatronics) with over 114 citations, an h-index of 6, and an i10-index of 5.
